Research Associate @ Bharat Institute

Jan 2026 – Present

Engineering Pseudomonas denitrificans cellular machinery for high-efficiency biosynthesis of biodegradable biopolymers.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Designing engineered genomic pathways to biosynthesize Poly(3-hydroxypropionate) [P(3HP)].
  • Utilizing industrial side-streams like **glycerol** and **gluconate** as primary carbon feeds to deliver carbon-neutral processing.
  • Scaling up fermentation optimization protocols to maximize biopolymer titers in laboratory bioreactors.

Research Fellow @ National Taiwan University

Nov 2024 – Nov 2025

Valorization of agricultural agricultural waste into premium structural Bacterial Cellulose (BC) for eco-friendly packaging.

Key Advancements & Deliverables:

  • Developed robust biological and chemical pretreatment models to convert **peanut shell waste** into a nutrient-rich media.
  • Modified cellulose nanofibrils through enzymatic and chemical cross-linking.
  • Achieved **~40% improvement in structural tensile strength** and exceptional thermal barriers, validating industrial packaging viability.
  • Awarded the **NSTC Taiwan Postdoctoral Scholarship** for bioreactor cultivation optimization.

Ph.D. Research Summary @ University of Ulsan

Mar 2019 – Feb 2024

Developing a **Microbial Cell Surface Display (MCSD)** platform for high-affinity heavy metal biosorption and synthesis of therapeutic nanoparticles.

Significant Discoveries:

  • **Genetic Engineering**: Engineered *E. coli* cells to display heavy-metal-binding peptides on outer membranes using anchoring systems (OmpC and YiaT).
  • **Bioremediation**: Achieved massive biological adsorption and selective retrieval of **Cobalt** and **Nickel** from toxic battery wastewater.
  • **Nanotechnology Calcination**: Heated metal-bound biomass to synthesize highly crystalline **Cobalt Oxide (Co3O4)** and **Nickel Oxide (NiO)** nanoparticles.
  • **Applications**: Applied biogenic nanoparticles to degrade antibiotic norfloxacin (90% removal in 90 min) and organic dyes. Demonstrated strong anti-cancer properties with an **IC50 of 59 μg/mL** against cancer cell lines.
